Overview

Unseen Interface is an experimental project that explores how and when intuitive gestures could perform well in the near future human-computer interaction scenario(IoE).

Right now the smartphone is the central hub for connecting smart devices to each other. Voice control is one of the ways in which we’re offered a more human interaction, but talking is not the only way in which we communicate. The developments of neural nets, depth cameras and radar open up an entirely new space for gesture-based interaction models.

When people feel cold, they may rub their hands to warm themselves. What if we take human subconscious and instinctive behaviors which indicates their needs as a way of “input”, letting machine understand what their purpose is and provide output base on that？

And by combining existing cameras on the phone, computer and other smart devices to co-building a single physical coordinate system, we can provide a more accurate detective space with greater coverage.

Unseen “Gesture” provides a more intuitive and efficient interaction with almost no learning cost, avoiding Midas-touch problem and enabling for a truly intelligent smart environment.
